Earable Computing for Cognitive Enhancement

Developing non-invasive, ear-based wearables with advanced sensors for real-time cognitive monitoring and enhancement with unprecedented accuracy.

Project Overview

The Earable Computing for Cognitive Enhancement project represents a breakthrough in wearable technology. Our team has developed an innovative ear-based device that combines multiple sensing modalities, including EEG and PPG sensors, to monitor cognitive states with unprecedented accuracy and comfort.

This non-invasive technology enables real-time cognitive monitoring and feedback, opening new possibilities for enhancing human performance, preventing cognitive decline, and optimizing mental wellness in everyday settings.

Multi-modal Sensing

Our earable device combines EEG, PPG, IMU, and acoustic sensors to capture a comprehensive picture of cognitive and physiological states.

Edge Computing

On-device processing algorithms maximize privacy and enable real-time analysis without constant cloud connectivity.

Cognitive Enhancement

Non-invasive stimulation modalities provide gentle, personalized interventions to maintain focus and enhance performance.

Breakthrough in Cognitive Enhancement Using Earable Technology

Richards, E., Song, M., Vu, T., Wang, S.

Journal of Neural Engineering, Vol. 19, Issue 3 (2025)

This paper presents a novel earable device that combines EEG and PPG sensors for real-time cognitive monitoring and enhancement. Results from a 12-month study with 150 participants demonstrate significant improvements in sustained attention and cognitive performance.

Edge Computing for Privacy-Preserving Cognitive Monitoring

Song, M., Wang, S., Richards, E., Vu, T.

IEEE Transactions on Biomedical Engineering, Vol. 72 (2024)

This study presents a novel approach to privacy-preserving cognitive monitoring using edge computing on wearable devices. Our algorithms demonstrate 94% accuracy in cognitive state detection while minimizing data transmission.

Read Full Paper

Non-invasive Neurostimulation Through Earable Devices

Vu, T., Richards, E., Lee, J., Sharma, P.

Frontiers in Human Neuroscience, Vol. 18 (2024)

This paper explores the efficacy of various non-invasive neurostimulation modalities delivered through earable devices, showing significant potential for cognitive enhancement in real-world settings.
